I am nervous. I am definitely nervous writing today's post. I don't know why but I can feel my anxiety level rise up. It feels I am still new to this concept even if I have been exposed to it for the past 3 years.

When humans undergo emotional distress like this, is it generic for a machine to undergo such obstructions as well?

Atleast we know the cause and take the steps to fix it.

But, can a non-living thing like computers fix their own issue?

My 2 years of graduate study was filled, dealing with machine learning, data, SQL, and cloud. I have had hands-on experience with basic machine learning models to building complex neural networks.

And, for all of this, I needed to muster up the courage.

The confidence, when something went wrong.

The assurance and belief that I will be able to debug and fix the issue.

Without me mentally being ready to face what was to come infront of me, I don't think I would have learned and become a forte in what I am doing.(atleast to my level)

In today's post, I am going to mention the things I faced when working with such models.

  • Gathering Data - Gathering high quality data is a must. Sometimes, these data come incomplete, noisy, and biased which might lead to poor model performance.

  • Model Selection - Another headache is choosing the right model for the use case. There are numerous algorithms and frameworks available, and selecting the best one requires deep domain knowledge.

  • Overfitting and Underfitting - Balancing the complexity of a model to prevent overfitting (where the model performs well on training data but poorly on unseen data) or underfitting (where the model is too simplistic to capture patterns) is a common challenge.

  • Computation - Training complex models often requires significant computational resources, including high-performance GPUs or TPUs.

  • Ethical Considerations - Machine learning engineers must consider ethical issues related to bias, fairness, and privacy when developing and deploying models. Biased models can perpetuate discrimination, and privacy breaches can lead to legal and reputational issues.

  • Deployment - It is mandatory that the trained model must be deployed properly in order to be accessed by other users or another time.
