"Vijay, what are we supposed to do with this information now?"

uhmm.... *rotates eyes* that is a Collocation guys! Tadaaaa

"Wait... how is that a collocation?"

So, Collocations are groups of two or more words that are commonly-used together. In English collocations are largely idiomatic. A collocation is considered “correct” only because its use has been widely-accepted by native English speakers.

When I said "heavy rain", the word "heavy" and "rain" frequently occur together. They are linked together and it means there is an intense amount of rain.

You wouldn't find anyone say, "massive rain" or "strong rain" to express this emotion/statement.

"Oh.. I get it now! So, Vijay, did you seek shelter?"

Oi, you understood well! (I hope you understand now that "seek shelter" is another Collocation example)

Manually, it is now easy to identify collocations. Aint it?

Can a computer identify these collocations?

It can! Ofcourse. With the help of NLP and its libraries, anything is possible in the text-world.

Now, why am I talking about Collocations?

It's because I related to it and I came across it in my real life. I was assigned with a sentiment analysis task in my NLP classroom by my professor, and during the text preprocessing step, I had to identify these Collocations and avoid it.

These words usually occur in terms of BiGrams or Trigrams.

Now, you are wondering, What are BiGrams and TriGrams?

Maybe, I can talk about that in another blog~ *I dont want to deviate from the topic*

For now, just understand, BiGrams contain word sets of 2 and TiGrams contain word sets of 3.

So, how are these collocations different from Bigrams and TiGrams?

Let's consider the example, "She loved machine learning as much as ice creams"

BiGrams - ["she","loved"],["loved","machine"],["machine","learning"],["learning,as"],["as,much"],["much,as"]

TriGrams - ["she","loved","machine"], ["loved","machine","learning"]

Collocations - ["machine learning"] and []"ice creams"]

In NLP, we can identify these Collocations with the help of NLTK library.
