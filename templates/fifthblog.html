Day 4 - (09/18/2023)

I had already mentioned that I am searching for jobs now, which means that I am applying to different types of jobs everyday. These applications seem like a full time job already and it is mentally draining.

I realised today that my job application > 200. This is a 6 month hardwork. I started applying long back in Dec 2022 like with seriousness. I spoke with different career coaches, tailored my resume and made sure everything was set!

Today, I opened the Excel sheet where I documented the information of all the companies I applied to and the respective roles that I had applied to. This helped me organize my thoughts so that I don't apply to the same job again twice. This also aided me in keeping the record of my progress in these 6 months.

Looking back, eventhough I felt lazy at times to write them down at that point of time, right now, it is helpful for me. As a data analyst, I had the perspective of analysing the data and maybe display vital trends and patterns in all of my applications.

The link to my Excel sheet is - Click Here

Interesting insights that I have extracted from my data is:

  • The overall success rate for job applications is 45%, indicating that nearly half of the applications result in a successful outcome.
  • Applications with referrals have a significantly higher success rate of 60% compared to those without referrals, which have a success rate of 40%. Referrals seem to play a crucial role in application success.
  • Over the past two years (2022 and 2023), there has been an increase in the number of job applications, interviews, and successful applications. However, there's also been a slight increase in rejections.
  • Among the companies applied to, Google, JP Morgan, and CVS Health have the highest success rates, all exceeding 70%.
  • Interviews have a conversion rate of 70% to successful applications, indicating that the majority of interviews lead to job offers.
  • While the overall rejection rate is 30%, it has decreased from 35% in 2022 to 25% in 2023, suggesting an improvement in the job application strategy.
  • Full-time applications have a higher success rate (50%) compared to internships (35%). It seems that full-time positions are more favorable for successful applications.
  • Applications with referrals not only have a higher success rate but also a higher chance of getting an interview, with a conversion rate of 80%.
  • The interview success rate has remained relatively stable between 2022 and 2023, with a slight increase from 65% to 70%.

    • The success rate of an application is calculated by the following formula -

    =(COUNTIF(C2:H2,"Yes") / COUNTA(C2:H2)) * 100
