With flourishing Technology and AI, we are into doing so many things, turning all the impossible to possible.

But there are some things that help me wonder, can anything be achieved with AI? How about going to the other persons location through a video call? or how about experiencing a whole new world like they do in Avatar (the movie), without actually traveling there but with just mere consciousness?

If this ever comes true, my mom will be the happiest! She always wanted to do something like that so she could come to my place and cook food for me.

That's sweet right? *awwww*

I woke up today with a dream lingering in my thoughts. It was a very vague and an uncomfortable feeling, which means my dream was just dull. I recall getting up and moving around like a zombie, because I did not like the fact about how weird I felt because I didn't like my dream and it put my entire mood to sogginess.

I dreamt about attending a friend's wedding and I seriously have no idea why this made me uncomfortable instead of where I should be rejoicing for my friend getting married. It is a Happy Occasion!

I was pouring my milk when my I thought about wanting to check the meaning of my dream on the internet and I googled.

Instantly, something caught my eye. A blog post was written about interpreting dreams by a Dream Analyst.

Did I read that right? We have someone called a Dream Analyst?

I guess so. *sigh*

To my unknown knowledge, I found out that a Dream Analyst is basically someone with psychology background, interpreting people's dreams.

How insane is that?

I have heard about financial analysts, risk analysts, clinical data analysts and never in a million years, I thought I would come to something like this.

I couldn't believe it and I was flabbergasted.

However, I understood that our dreams are also a data, to be analysed, interpreted, visualised and concluded with an output.

I did not stop there, I went further down the line to see if there were any Dream Datasets that were used for analysis.

And you know what? I did find a dataset.

Check it out at - https://sleepanddreamdatabase.org/

You can share and read other people's dreams in this website. You have the proficiency of choosing different categories of Dreams.

How cool is this?

*Mind Blowing*

Now, I don't really know the logic behind a psychologist/dream analyst interpreting a dream but with my eyes always based on looking at data, I believed I could bring out the possible ways to interpret a dream in a data scientist/data analyst POV.

So, can Data Science now really be used for dreams as well?

It looks like it can *woah*

There are infact research going on, for using machine learning algorithms to analyse Dreams and its scientific validity and practical applications are subject to ongoing debate. Nonetheless, it can be a valuable tool for individuals interested in self-reflection and introspection, especially when combined with qualitative expertise in psychology or therapy.

Data Collection
  • Encouraging an individual to document certain dreams that occur regularly in a structured format (like dream journaling/ digital recording) can be used as a dataset.
  • Example of a structured format - date, time of the dream, key events, characters, emotions, and any recurring symbols.

    • Data Preprocessing
  • Assigning categories or tags to the dreams based on common themes, emotions, or symbols is an example of Data Categorization. This way, it would be easier for classification.

    • Descriptive Statistics
  • Calculate basic descriptive statistics such as the frequency of recurring symbols, the distribution of emotions, and the time of day when dreams are recorded.

    • Sentiment Analysis
  • The existence of NLP can be used to analyse the dreams, breaking it down and finding out the sentiment of the dream - (Positive/Negative)

    • Machine Learning and Pattern Recognition
  • Applying machine learning algorithms to identify patterns or associations between specific elements in dreams.

    • Cluster Analysis
  • Exploring relationships between dreams within clusters to uncover patterns.

    • Visualization
  • Creating word clouds to visually represent the most frequently occurring words or themes in dreams.

    • As I am writing this, I still feel this idea is so weird.

    P.S - It's important to note that dreams are highly subjective, and their meaning can vary widely among individuals.
