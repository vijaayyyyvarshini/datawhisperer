    What is VARNA? (you will definitely ask this question)

    Let me explain.. *ahem ahem, clears throat*

    VARNA (Visualization Applet for RNA) is a software tool designed to visualize RNA secondary structures. It provides a graphical representation of RNA molecules, highlighting the base pairings and secondary structure motifs.

    Logic?

    The logic behind VARNA is to make it easier for researchers and scientists to understand and analyze the complex folding patterns of RNA molecules.

    The RNA secondary structure prediction involves the determination of how RNA molecules fold into various structural elements, such as helices (stem-loop structures), bulges, internal loops, and hairpin loops.

    Wow! That is interesting Vijay.. So, how what are the steps?

    RNA Sequence:

  • I input an RNA sequence into VARNA. This sequence can be obtained from experiments or generated computationally.

    • Structure Prediction:

  • VARNA can predict the secondary structure of the RNA molecule based on the input sequence. It uses algorithms and energy minimization techniques to determine which bases form base pairs and how they interact.

    • Representation:

  • Once the secondary structure is predicted, VARNA generates a graphical representation of the RNA molecule. It displays the RNA sequence as a linear strand, and it highlights base pairs and structural motifs using color-coding and line shapes.

    • Interactive Visualization:

    ARNA allows users to interact with the graphical representation. They can zoom in and out, pan, and rotate the structure to explore different regions of the RNA molecule.

    Energy

    The energy value of associated with an RNA secondary structure represents the thermodynamic stability or free energy of that particular structure. In the context of RNA secondary structures, this energy value indicates how favorable or stable the folding of the RNA molecule into the given structure is.

    For my above protein sequence, my Energy output was -0.9.


    A negative energy value, like -0.9 in my example, suggests that the RNA secondary structure is thermodynamically stable. In other words, it is energetically favorable for the RNA molecule to adopt this specific folding pattern.

    Well, that sounds good? Doesn't it? We don't want an unstable protein structure.
