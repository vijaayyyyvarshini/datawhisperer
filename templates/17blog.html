How many of you knew that you could request LinkedIn for a copy of your own data?

I got to know this late. Up until a few weeks before, I was playing around with my LinkedIn pages and realized that I could get my account data.

I knew for a reason that I needed to do something with this data.

and, my mind went to - Tableau Dashboard!

'Hey, we have seen so many people already do this. It is nothing new.'

No!

Not two DNA can be similar.

My brain has also worked out some of the Statistical Interpretations.

Tree Map - Number of people in each company:

  • The data shows a high variance, with certain companies or universities (like Saint Louis University) having a significantly higher representation than others.

    • Number of companies applied for in a particular day via LinkedIn:

  • The modal (most frequent) number of applications in a day seems to be around 2-3, as multiple dates have these values.

    • Number of Messages Received:

  • There's a wide spread, with values ranging from 14 to 99 messages.
  • The distribution seems right-skewed, with fewer people receiving very high messages and more people receiving fewer messages.

    • Number of people with the same first name:

  • The data spread from 1 to 5, is not widespread but shows a variance in name popularity.
  • Again, it seems right-skewed, with fewer names being very common and many names being less common.

    • Number of connections in each week:

  • With the running total graph, it looks like there's an upward trend in the number of connections over time.
  • Initially, the growth seems stable but starts to increase rapidly around mid-May. This suggests some external factors or efforts might have led to a boost in connections.
  • The time (in weeks) and the number of connections are positively correlated, meaning as time progresses, the number of connections tends to increase
